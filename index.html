East West University Computer Programming Club

Annual Roadmap 2025-2026

Prepared for: Club Moderators: Dr. Taskeed Jabid, Professor; Dr. Mohammad Manzurul Islam, Assistant Professor, Department of Computer Science & Engineering

Prepared by: Ahnaf Shahadat Taseen, Volunteer Executive

Date: July 13, 2025

About the Author

Hello! I'm Ahnaf Shahadat Taseen, a Volunteer Executive at the East West University Computer Programming Club. This roadmap is a culmination of our vision for the club's growth and impact in the coming year. My passion lies in fostering a vibrant community where students can excel in competitive programming, explore cutting-edge fields like Data Science and AI, and engage in meaningful research. I believe this plan will significantly contribute to the development of our members and the club as a whole.

🚀 Enhancing Competitive Programming Excellence

This section focuses on strengthening the club's competitive programming foundation, aiming to cultivate a skilled and enthusiastic community of problem-solvers.

  • Member Categorization: Divide general members into three distinct categories based on their current competitive programming proficiency (e.g., beginners, intermediate, advanced). This allows for tailored training and mentorship.
  • Inspiration and Motivation: Implement initiatives to inspire and motivate general members towards competitive programming. This could include showcasing success stories, inviting experienced competitive programmers for talks, and highlighting the benefits of competitive programming in academic and career development.
  • Structured Sessions: Organize regular, structured competitive programming sessions. These sessions will cover fundamental algorithms, data structures, and problem-solving techniques relevant to competitive programming.
  • Consistent Contests: Conduct frequent internal programming contests to provide members with practical experience, track their progress, and foster a competitive yet supportive environment. These contests will be designed to challenge students at various skill levels.

📊 Establishing a Data Science & AI Wing

Recognizing the growing importance of Data Science and Artificial Intelligence, this new sector will empower members with knowledge and practical skills in these cutting-edge fields.

  • AI Tool Familiarization: Introduce general members to various AI tools, demonstrating their functionalities and practical applications across different domains. Focus will be on "how to use" these tools effectively for skill development.
  • Data Science Fundamentals for Freshers: Develop a curriculum specifically designed for fresher students to grasp core data science concepts, making the subject accessible and engaging from the outset.
  • Project-Based Learning: Create interest in data science by encouraging and facilitating the creation of data science projects. This hands-on approach will solidify understanding and foster innovation.
  • "Datathon" Events: Organize "Datathon" events to challenge members with real-world data science problems, allowing them to apply their knowledge and develop their skills in a collaborative and competitive setting.
  • Knowledge Sharing Initiatives: Conduct regular sessions, seminars, classes, and workshops on various data science and AI topics. Conclude these initiatives with project showcasing events to highlight member achievements and foster a culture of innovation.

📝 Fostering Research Paper Involvement

This section aims to demystify the research paper process and encourage student participation in academic research and publication.

  • Research Inspiration: Inspire students to engage in research by highlighting the impact of research, showcasing successful student research projects, and demonstrating how research contributes to innovation.
  • Publication Awareness: Educate students about the research paper publication process, including journal selection, submission guidelines, and peer review.
  • Research Paper Writing Workshops: Conduct workshops focused on the practical aspects of writing a research paper, including structuring, literature review, methodology, results, and discussion.
  • Collaboration with Faculty: Guide students on how to effectively communicate and collaborate with faculty members regarding research paper ideas and potential publications, fostering mentorship opportunities.
  • Hands-on Research Experience: Organize workshops and provide resources that allow students to gain practical experience in working on research papers, from ideation to data analysis and writing.

🌐 Revitalizing the Club Website

The existing club website will be transformed into a dynamic and informative platform, serving as the central hub for club activities and information.

  • Content Development: Actively develop the website by adding comprehensive information about the club's activities, initiatives, and achievements.
  • Regular Updates: Establish a routine for regularly updating the website with news about seminars, workshops, contests, and other club events.
  • Showcasing Club Impact: Utilize the website to effectively showcase the club's impact and the diverse opportunities it offers to members.

📱 Developing a Club Management Mobile Application

A dedicated mobile application will be developed to provide a clear and centralized overview of club operations and member engagement.

  • Membership Dashboard: The app will feature a comprehensive dashboard providing club moderators with real-time insights into the total number of active members.
  • Sector-wise Member Visualization: The dashboard will offer clear visualizations of member distribution across different sectors (e.g., competitive programming, research, data science, video editing), allowing for informed decision-making and resource allocation.
  • Enhanced Club Management: This application will serve as a crucial tool for efficient club management, offering a transparent and organized view of member engagement and club dynamics.

📧 Professionalizing Club Communication via Email

The EWUCOPC official email ID will be utilized more effectively to streamline communication and professionalize outreach.

  • Improved Email Management: Implement a robust system for managing and organizing club emails, ensuring timely responses and efficient communication.
  • Member Communication Hub: Leverage the club email for all official communication with general members, including important notices, event updates, and announcements.
  • Professional Correspondence: Utilize the official email ID for all external correspondence, establishing a professional and consistent brand image for the club.

This roadmap provides a comprehensive plan for the East West University Computer Programming Club for the upcoming year. Each initiative is designed to enhance member skills, foster innovation, and strengthen the club's presence within the university community.
